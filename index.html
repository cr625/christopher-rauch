Christopher Rauch

PhD Candidate in Information Science | LLM & Rule-Based Reasoning Research | Juris Doctor (Rutgers Law School)

Researcher exploring the integration of large language models with rule-based reasoning systems, applying software development expertise to information science challenges. My work bridges AI capabilities with structured knowledge representation, informed by principles of formal reasoning.

My research focuses on ethical decision modeling with AI methods and domain-specific algorithmic decision-making. This interdisciplinary approach draws on both technical knowledge and ethical frameworks to address complex challenges in AI development and implementation.

Projects

ProEthica

An LLM-based ethical decision-making framework incorporating domain ontologies and role-based ethics for precise reasoning about entities, relationships, and professional guidelines.

Using domain ontologies to create structured knowledge, ProEthica evaluates decisions through professional roles bound by specific guidelines. It combines ontological reasoning with LLM capabilities via Model Context Protocol to ensure valid input/output structures and enhance ethical reasoning with structured domain knowledge.
